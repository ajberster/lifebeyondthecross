Life Beyond the Cross Program

An immersive teaching journey through identity, calling, and kingdom purpose

About the Program

The Life Beyond the Cross Program is a structured teaching journey designed to help believers move from knowing about Christ to walking in the fullness of who they are in Him. It is built on the conviction that what was accomplished at the Cross and through the Resurrection is not just a historical event — it is a present reality that every believer is invited to inhabit.

The program draws together the core threads of the Life Beyond the Cross teaching ministry: the believer's identity in Christ, the power and presence of the Holy Spirit, the role of the five-fold ministry gifts, the practice of biblical meditation and journaling, and the call to take your place in building the kingdom of God on earth.

What the Program Covers

  1. Your New Identity — Who you are now that you are in Christ: recreated, redeemed, and seated in heavenly places.
  2. The Holy Spirit Within You — The indwelling presence, power, and anointing of the Holy Spirit in the believer's daily life.
  3. Resurrection Power — Accessing and walking in the same power that raised Jesus from the dead.
  4. Hearing God's Voice — Developing sensitivity to the still small voice and the Spirit's leading.
  5. Meditation and Revelation — Biblical meditation as a key to unlocking revelation knowledge and spiritual growth.
  6. The Five-Fold Gifts — Understanding the ministry gifts given by Christ and discovering your own calling within the body.
  7. Your Kingdom Assignment — Identifying and stepping into the specific role God has designed for you in building His church on earth.

Who Is This For?

The program is designed for any born-again believer who senses that there is more — more power, more purpose, more fruitfulness — than they are currently experiencing. You do not need a theological background or prior ministry experience. You need a willing heart and a hunger for the things of God.

It is suitable for individual study, small groups, or church-based discipleship programs.
